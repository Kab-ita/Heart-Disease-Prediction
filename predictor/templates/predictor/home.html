1. ID3 Logic

Constructed independently, the ID3 engine partitions information by computing continuous features into high-gain categorical breaks using greedy structural entropy reductions.

2. C4.5 Logic

Improves on raw entropy by applying Gain Ratio penalties to clear attribute splitting biases, compiling descriptive rule-based execution footprints and tracing individual prediction tracks.

3. Random Forest Voter

A scikit-learn ensemble used purely as a third, independent vote. When all three agree, that's the strongest signal.

Diagnostic Attribute Reference Guide

UCI Schema Protocol
  • Chest Pain Type (ChestPainType)

    Categorized as Typical Angina (TA), Atypical Angina (ATA), Non-Anginal Pain (NAP), or Asymptomatic (ASY). ASY nodes frequently highlight structural ischemic conditions.

  • Resting Blood Pressure (RestingBP)

    Patient's resting cardiovascular pressure in mm Hg. Elevated levels (>=140 mm Hg) steer classification pathways toward multi-system hypertensive load limits.

  • Serum Cholesterol (Cholesterol)

    Total blood lipid counts tracked in mg/dl. High cholesterol levels represent key nodes for mapping atherosclerotic plaque risks.

  • ST Depression (Oldpeak)

    Tracks depression stress values measured relative to rest levels. Higher numerical shifts indicate distinct sub-endocardial injury vectors.

  • Peak ST/T Slope (ST_Slope)

    The geometric slope of the stress ECG segment (Up, Flat, or Down). Flat or downwards sloping segments serve as strong terminal node triggers for structural disease classification.

  • Max Heart Rate (MaxHR)

    Peak beats achieved during stress examinations. Reduced limits correlate mathematically with diminished cardiovascular cardiac reserve output capacity.
